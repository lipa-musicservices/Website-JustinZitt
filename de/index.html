About

Clear writing, open form, and a sound that stays close to the listener.

Justin Zitt is a German jazz pianist and composer currently based in Amsterdam. After early support through the Jazz Juniors program in Baden-Württemberg and preparatory studies with Hubert Nuss in Stuttgart, he completed his Bachelor in Jazz Piano in Mannheim, where he studied with Rainer Böhm and Lorenz Kellhuber. He is now continuing his artistic path in an international environment, working between composition, improvisation, and collaborative performance.

His playing is marked by clarity, patience, and a strong sense of narrative. Rather than choosing between structure and freedom, Justin works at the point where both can support each other: composed material remains flexible, and improvisation becomes part of the form rather than decoration around it.

In recent years, he has developed his profile through ensemble work, original projects, and an expanding network of collaborations. His artistic focus lies in music that is refined without becoming distant, detailed without becoming academic, and open enough to let performance shape the final result.

With FRIGO, Justin presents a debut statement that reflects exactly that approach: thoughtful, spacious, and grounded in a contemporary piano trio language with its own voice.

Release

FRIGO

FRIGO is Justin Zitt’s debut album and a focused piano trio release with Julian Grüneberg and Julius Steyer. The record balances compositional precision with real openness in performance: melodies remain clear, rhythms breathe, and the trio creates space rather than filling every corner. The result is music that feels contemporary without chasing fashion — thoughtful, agile, and quietly intense.

Release Debut album, released via Unit Records in 2025.
Line-up Justin Zitt — piano, Julian Grüneberg — bass, Julius Steyer — drums.
Character Modern piano trio music with lyrical focus, rhythmic detail, and spacious interplay.

Projects

Writing, trio work, and collaboration.

Justin’s work moves across original trio repertoire, collaborative ensemble settings, and project-based formats in which composition and improvisation meet on equal terms. The common thread is always the same: musical clarity, openness in performance, and a sound that is precise without becoming rigid.

Piano Trio

A central format in Justin’s work, bringing together composition, interaction, and dynamic elasticity in a concise acoustic setting.

Original Composition

Music shaped by strong melodic material, structural awareness, and a preference for depth over effect.

Collaborative Work

Ensemble projects built on listening, shared responsibility, and the belief that the best music rarely comes from someone trying too hard to be impressive.
